Current projects I am working on:

  1. Spatial-Temporal Analysis/Survival Analysis: In survival analysis for a disease, we counted the time a person had an event, and then we analyze the data. Notice that censor, patients dropped the study, is the main issue that survival analysis is different from linear regression. In the longitudinal analysis, we analyze the outcome based on what was happening during the time course for the outcomes. When we combine both survival and longitudinal analysis, we have a model called joint longitudinal survival analysis. In those models, they could capture what happened and the survival time of patients according to the changes in their body reactions.
    So, why would I like to study Spatial-temporal survival analysis? One of the reasons is the health determinant factors in each location will affect the health of each patient. For example, people living in polluted areas by oil refined regions will have different survival time from ones living in a clean environment for cancer patients. And people living near that contaminated area may have related outcomes. Our models will be able to capture those factors.
  2. Does increasing continuity of care protect patients with chronic diseases from Emergency visits or hospitalizations? In this project, we would like to answer the question by analyzing the data of patients living in Hinds county and its neighbors of Mississippi by applying Spatial-temporal analysis.
  3. Neural Networks in Health Science: Neuron Network or Convolution Neuron Network works very well in image classification. Some studies used successfully Neural Network in predicting Alzheimer's diseases from MRI images of patients in which they could outpace human predictions by six months. Can we build models that join Neural Network techniques and patients' medical records to get better results for predictions and also for preventions?
